Introduction: The Evolution of Version Control

Version Control Systems (VCS) are essential tools for tracking changes in files (typically source code) over time.

They enable multi-person collaboration, preserve a history of modifications, and allow reversion to previous versions. The evolution of these systems has progressed from early, server-based centralized models to modern distributed systems.
